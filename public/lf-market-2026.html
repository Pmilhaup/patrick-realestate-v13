02 · The Headline
The Number

For the first time in five years,
sellers are receiving over asking.

Through May of 2026, the average Lake Forest luxury home (closed price at or above one million dollars) settled at 100.2% of its original list price. That is not a sliver. That is, at scale, the entire luxury segment of one of the most discerning markets in the Midwest clearing for more than what its sellers initially asked.

100.2%

A house that commands its asking price is also a house that has been correctly priced.
The market is rewarding accurate sellers in 2026 — not optimistic ones.

03 · The Trailing Twelve
The Last Twelve Months

Volume up. Days down.
List price met.

The single-number story on the previous page is supported by every metric beneath it. Across the trailing twelve months, the Lake Forest luxury market did not merely improve — it shifted decisively in the seller’s direction along every dimension that matters at the closing table.

Read the table top to bottom. Volume is up by more than a quarter year-over-year, on twenty-three percent more closings — meaning the market is absorbing more homes at the same higher prices it was already paying. Time on market has dropped by nearly a third. And the sale-to-list ratio — the single most reliable measure of which side of the table is being met — has tightened toward the listing side by 170 basis points over the same period.

04 · The Practice
Discerning Representation

The reading is the easy part.
The placement is the practice.

A market window is only useful to the seller who knows how to step through it. The data on the previous pages describes the corridor; it does not describe a particular home, a particular street, or a particular buyer. That work — connecting the market signal to the closing table — is what discerning sellers ask of their advisor.
